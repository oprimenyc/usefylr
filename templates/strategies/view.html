Strategy Overview

Based on your responses, you may be eligible for an expanded home office deduction. This strategy involves properly calculating the percentage of your home used for business and maximizing all related expenses.

The home office deduction is available for both homeowners and renters, and applies to all types of homes. For your business profile as a technology consultant working primarily from home, this deduction represents one of the most accessible tax benefits available.

Implementation Steps
  1. Measure your dedicated home office space

    Accurately measure the square footage of your dedicated office area. This must be a space used regularly and exclusively for business.

  2. Calculate the percentage of your home used for business

    Divide your office square footage by your home's total square footage to determine the business percentage.

  3. Track all related expenses

    Keep thorough records of all expenses including:

    • Mortgage interest or rent
    • Property taxes
    • Homeowners/renters insurance
    • Utilities (electricity, gas, water)
    • Internet and phone service
    • Home maintenance and repairs
    • Depreciation (if you own)

  4. Choose the right calculation method

    Compare the simplified method ($5 per square foot, up to 300 sq ft) against the regular method (based on actual expenses) to determine which provides the larger deduction.

  5. Maintain thorough documentation

    Take photos of your home office, keep receipts for all expenses, and document the business use of the space in case of audit.

Requirements & Considerations
  • Regular and exclusive use

    The area must be used exclusively for business. A guest bedroom that occasionally houses visitors would not qualify.

  • Principal place of business

    The home office must be your principal place of business, used regularly for business activities.

  • Direct vs. indirect expenses

    Direct expenses (like painting only the office) are 100% deductible. Indirect expenses (like utilities) are deductible based on the percentage of home used for business.

  • Depreciation recapture

    If you own your home and claim depreciation, you may face depreciation recapture when you sell your home.
