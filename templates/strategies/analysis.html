Home Office Deduction Maximizer
$2,450 Potential Savings
Strategy Overview

Based on your responses, you may be eligible for an expanded home office deduction. This strategy involves properly calculating the percentage of your home used for business and maximizing all related expenses.

Implementation Steps
  1. Accurately measure your dedicated home office space
  2. Calculate the percentage of your home used for business
  3. Track all related expenses (utilities, internet, insurance, etc.)
  4. Use the simplified method ($5/sq ft) or the regular method based on your situation
  5. Maintain thorough documentation including photos and expense records
Requirements & Considerations
  • Space must be used regularly and exclusively for business
  • If using the regular method, indirect expenses are prorated based on percentage of home used
  • Direct expenses (like paint for office only) are 100% deductible
  • Proper documentation is essential in case of audit
Important: This strategy requires regular and exclusive use of the designated area for business. Occasional personal use may disqualify the deduction.
Vehicle Expense Optimization
$3,275 Potential Savings
Strategy Overview

Based on your business activities, you may benefit from optimizing your vehicle expense deductions. Comparing the standard mileage rate versus actual expenses can lead to significant tax savings.

Implementation Steps
  1. Keep detailed mileage logs (date, destination, purpose, miles)
  2. Track all vehicle expenses (gas, insurance, repairs, depreciation)
  3. Calculate deduction using both methods (standard rate vs. actual)
  4. Choose the method that maximizes your deduction
  5. Document business purpose for all trips
Requirements & Considerations
  • If using standard mileage rate for a leased vehicle, you must use it for the entire lease period
  • If using actual expenses, track business use percentage carefully
  • Consider Section 179 deduction for vehicle purchases over 6,000 pounds
  • Commuting miles are generally not deductible
Important: Detailed contemporaneous records are critical. The IRS often scrutinizes vehicle deductions during audits.

Strategy Overview

Implementation Steps
  1. Establish a Solo 401(k) plan before December 31
  2. Make employee contributions up to $22,500 (2023 limit)
  3. Add employer profit-sharing contributions up to 25% of compensation
  4. Total combined contributions can reach $66,000 (2023 limit)
  5. Consider Roth options for diversified tax treatment
Requirements & Considerations

  • Plan must be established by December 31, but contributions for employer portion can be made until tax filing
  • Required annual filing of Form 5500-EZ once plan assets exceed $250,000
  • Can be combined with health savings accounts (HSA) for additional tax benefits
Important: Contribution limits are subject to change annually. Consult with a financial advisor to maximize contributions while staying compliant.
